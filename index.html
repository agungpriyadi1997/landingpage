Mountain

Mountains are parts of the earth's crust that are located higher than the surrounding area. Mountains typically have steep sides that expose significant amounts of bedrock. Mountains differ from plateaus in that their peak areas are limited; a mountain larger than a hill, usually having a height of at least 300 meters (1,000 feet) above the surrounding land. Some mountains are stand-alone peaks, but most are part of mountain ranges

Everest Mountain

Mount Everest is the second highest mountain in the world after Mauna Kea when measured from sea level.

Fuji Mountain

This mountain is the second highest volcano located on an island on the Asian continent (after Mount Kerinci on Sumatra Island), and the seventh highest peak of an island on Earth.
